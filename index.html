The Stranger Didn't Know

The Stranger didn't know...
He lived in a rock
With eyes wide open
Yet very little thought
Of what is around
And which one to sort.
The inside or the picture
In front of the dazing eyes
With a sense of wonder
A little truth, And so many lies.
The Stranger didn't know...
So he threw a rock
What looked like a lump
Turned into a croc.
He has to swim
Without any fins
Felt like in a cage
With tired old wings.
The Stranger didn't know...
So for freedom he screamed
Raging why he can't
Till he noticed a shadow
Of another like him, burnt.
There's neither pain or joy
Just like a passing thought
A glimpse of all that's there
And what could be lost.
The dead doesn't talk
Even when lighting strikes
So he felt alive, breathing
More than his would have liked.
He sat on the land
Under the starry night's glow
The Stranger knew there are things
More than he would ever know.
